How to Find Affordable Bankruptcy Legal Help

You do not have to choose between no lawyer and an expensive one. Several options exist for affordable bankruptcy legal help:

  • Legal aid organizations: Nonprofits provide free bankruptcy representation to low-income individuals. Find your local legal aid at lawhelp.org or lsc.gov.
  • Law school clinics: Many law schools operate bankruptcy clinics where students (supervised by licensed attorneys) handle cases for free or minimal cost.
  • Limited scope representation: Some attorneys offer "unbundled" services -- they help with specific parts of your case (reviewing your schedules, attending the 341 meeting) at a lower cost than full representation.
  • Flat fee attorneys: Look for attorneys who charge flat fees rather than hourly. Get quotes from at least 3 attorneys.
  • Bar association referrals: Your local bar association maintains a lawyer referral service that can connect you with attorneys who offer reduced-fee consultations.

Red flags for mill firms: Be cautious of firms advertising "no money down" Chapter 13 or extremely low fees. High-volume bankruptcy mills often provide minimal attention, make errors, and charge fees at or above the court-approved maximum. Check reviews and ask about caseload.
